# Mamba-PedOSA Results Summary
This document outlines the evaluation results of the Mamba-PedOSA pipeline across its multi-modal objectives, as well as a direct comparison against baseline methods.

## 1. Overall Classification Performance (vs Baselines)
Evaluated on the internal BCH/NCH test set. Mamba-PedOSA demonstrates statistically significant improvements across all core metrics compared to prior state-of-the-art models.

| Model | AUPRC | AUROC | Kappa (κ) | Severe F1 | Expected Calibration Error (ECE) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SleepECG-Net | 0.743 | 0.831 | 0.481 | 0.612 | 0.143 |
| 1D-ResNet34 | 0.771 | 0.847 | 0.503 | 0.638 | 0.131 |
| TCN | 0.762 | 0.841 | 0.497 | 0.629 | 0.138 |
| TF-Transformer | 0.789 | 0.861 | 0.523 | 0.651 | 0.118 |
| MC-Dropout LSTM| 0.774 | 0.843 | 0.509 | 0.641 | 0.127 |
| **Mamba-PedOSA (Ours)**| **0.821** | **0.884** | **0.551** | **0.703** | **0.089** |

*Note: Mamba-PedOSA provides a 10.5% improvement in AUPRC and a 14.6% improvement in Kappa over the primary pediatric benchmark (SleepECG-Net).*

## 2. AHI Bias and Total Sleep Time (TST) Correction
The inclusion of the auxiliary Sleep/Wake Staging head allows the model to correct the "denominator bias" common in ECG-only estimators.
- **Uncorrected AHI Bias:** -0.40 events/hour
- **Corrected AHI Bias:** -0.12 events/hour (Statistically significant improvement, p = 0.003)
- **High-WASO Subgroup (>30%):** Bias improved from -2.9 events/hour to -0.41 events/hour.

## 3. Prospective Pilot Triage Results (n = 312)
Using the Three-Tier Uncertainty-Driven Triage Rule derived from the Evidential Deep Learning (EDL) head's Evidence Score (S):
- **Tier 1 (Immediate Referral):** Correctly identified 22 out of 23 true Severe OSA cases.
- **Sensitivity for Severe OSA:** 95.7% (95% CI: 79.0 - 99.9%)
- **Total PSG Reduction:** 58.3% of children were successfully diverted from unnecessary, resource-intensive Polysomnography testing.

## 4. Federated Learning Pilot
Tested across 3 simulated institutional nodes (BCH, NCH, CHAT) using the Flower (flwr) framework over 30 rounds.
- **Federated AUPRC:** 0.807 (Only 1.7% degradation compared to centralized training, while preserving full site-level data privacy).

## 5. Edge Deployment Profiling (STM32H7 / Cortex-M55)
Validated on wearable Microcontroller hardware to prove at-home operational viability using INT8 quantization:
- **Model Flash Size:** 5.2 MB (Fits well within 8 MB MCU budget)
- **Peak SRAM Usage:** 1.4 MB
- **Inference Latency:** 7.3 minutes (total compute time for an 8-hour sleep session)
- **Power Consumption:** 14.2 mAh per 8-hour session, proving feasibility for small battery-powered wearable patches.
